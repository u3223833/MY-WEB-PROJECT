Robin Boyd

The Man Behind Modern Australian Architecture

Robin Gerard Penleigh Boyd (1919–1971) was an Australian architect, writer, and critic. He is best known for his dedication to functional modern design and his critiques of Australian architectural identity.

Boyd’s book The Australian Ugliness (1960) challenged conventional aesthetics, sparking debates about the nation’s suburban landscape. His forward-thinking approach continues to influence architects today.

This website explores his life, architectural philosophies, and notable projects that remain a testament to his innovative vision.

This website was produced by students in the Faculty of Arts & Design, University of Canberra, 2025.
